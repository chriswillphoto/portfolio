About Me

Raised on the mean streets of a cushy, middle-class, north shore suburb, I draw on a wide and varied life to bring perspective and passion to everything I work on.

I started to learn to code out of curiosity and quickly found that it utilised two of my favourite things: problem solving and creative thinking. The simple act of writing my first web page had me hooked and fiending for more. I found it was something I could definitely do all day, every day, which led me to learning web development at General Assembly.

I live a life filled with doing random things for shits and giggles, because no one ever found growth by sitting inside their comfort zone. I tend to throw myself face first into things, like an experiential crash test dummy, just to see how things shake out.

This is just a first draft and 'selling yourself' feels dirty. Stop Judging Me.
